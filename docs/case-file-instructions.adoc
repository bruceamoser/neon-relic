== Before You Begin

A Case File is a reactive operations board, not a scripted adventure. You are building a network of opportunities and pressures that the agents will navigate on their own terms. If your case can only be explained as a linear plot, it is not ready.

This guide walks you through building a complete Case File from scratch. For the full rules and mechanical detail, see the _Case File_ chapter and _DA Guidance_ chapter of the core rulebook.

*What you will need:*

* Blank templates for the case file package (see <<case-file-package>> in Chapter 15): Case File brief, Case Brief, Operations Board, Organization Reference, Relic Sheet, Location Pages, NPC Cards, and Information Cards
* The core rulebook for reference
* About 15–30 minutes of prep time

---

== Step 1: Choose the Artifact

Everything starts with the artifact. Before you think about location, NPCs, or plot, decide what the object is and what it does.

.Decide these first:
. *Mundane appearance* — What does it look like if you didn't know what it was?
. *Tier* — How dangerous is it? (Tier 1–4)
. *Risk Tag* — Stable, Volatile, Catastrophic, or Unknown?
. *Activation condition* — What triggers its power?
. *Core effect* — What does it actually do when activated?
. *Fracture consequence* — What goes wrong on an Artifact Die roll of 1?
. *Corruption cost* — How much Corruption does activation inflict? (+1 to +3 by tier)

If you are stuck, roll on the *d66 Artifact Type* table in the Case File chapter.

.TIP
****
The artifact's logic should flavor everything else. If the artifact is about command, NPCs should argue about authority. If it is about memory, witnesses should repeat themselves without knowing it. Let the artifact define the mood.
****

---

== Step 2: Write the Mystery Statement

Summarize the entire case in one sentence using this template:

[quote]
____
A [Tier X artifact] has surfaced in [Location]. If left uncontained, it will [relic outcome]. Meanwhile [Organization 1] and [Organization 2] are moving on the same problem. The agents need [Containment Truths] before they can recover it safely.
____

Write this on the Case Brief under *Mystery Statement*. If it takes more than one sentence, the case is too diffuse — simplify.

---

== Step 3: Define Containment Truths

Containment Truths are the facts the agents need to learn before they can safely resolve the case. Write three:

. *Trigger* — What makes the artifact more active or dangerous?
. *Appetite* — What does the artifact feed on, amplify, or attract?
. *Quiescence* — What conditions make it safe to handle, transport, or contain?

These truths become the real objectives of the investigation. The agents are not just finding the artifact — they are learning how to survive it.

.HARD RULE
****
Every Containment Truth must be available from at least *two field sources* (any combination of Locations and NPCs) plus an *HQ fallback* (see <<hq-safety-valve>> in Chapter 15). If only one source can reveal a truth, your case has a single point of failure. Redesign it.
****

---

== Step 4: Build the Operations Board — Relic Milestones

Relic milestones define what the artifact itself does if no one intervenes. They are keyed to specific phase numbers on the Operations Board's phases row. When the DA fills a shift quadrant that crosses a relic milestone, the artifact event triggers immediately.

. *Determine the total phases* — a 14-day case has 56 phases (14 days × 4 shifts). Phase 56 is the start; Phase 1 is catastrophe.
. *Write 4–5 milestones* keyed to descending phase numbers — each one describes an escalation.
. *Phase 1* is the catastrophe — the worst-case outcome if the case runs too long.
. *Include cross-link consequences* — does a relic milestone also advance an organization row?

Make each milestone specific to the artifact's logic. Don't write generic "things get spooky." If the artifact is about wounds, milestones should involve unexplained injuries appearing. If it is about sound, milestones should involve interference, phantom broadcasts, or compulsive listening.

.Example milestones (for a command-type relic):
* *Phase 44:* Handlers report unusual certainty and conviction.
* *Phase 32:* Witnesses start using the same phrases without coordination. Advance O3 (Police) by 1 square.
* *Phase 20:* Electronics fail near the object; radios broadcast static. O5 (Media) activates if still dormant.
* *Phase 8:* The artifact begins selecting a bearer.
* *Phase 1:* Catastrophe — public manifestation, mass obedience event, or violent claimant emergence.

---

== Step 5: Create Your Organization Rows

Organizations are external reactive pressures — rival factions, police, media, clergy, or any other force pushing on the same situation. Each organization is a countdown row on the Operations Board.

For each organization:

. *Name it* — Compact Recovery, Police Inquiry, Media Exposure, Church Custody, etc.
. *Set a starting value* (typically 6–12). The DA pre-fills squares from column 14 inward to this value.
. *Mark it Active or Dormant.* Dormant organizations are pre-printed on the board with the Active checkbox unchecked.
. *Write 3–5 milestones* using O#M# shorthand (e.g., O1M1, O1M2).
. *List triggers* — what advances this row (time, noise, publicity, violence, missed opportunities).
. *Write cross-link consequences in milestone text* — when this organization hits a milestone, include instructions to advance other rows or trigger relic milestone effects.
. *Note player-facing signs* — what can the agents notice before or as it worsens?

.Aim for:
* 2–3 *Active Organizations* (already in motion when the case starts)
* 0–2 *Dormant Organizations* (activated by specific events during play)
* Maximum of ~8 organization rows total

.IMPORTANT
****
If an organization cannot produce visible signs before it becomes a problem, it will feel arbitrary to the players. Always give them something to notice.
****

---

== Step 6: Author Case Entities

Case entities are independent building blocks, not sequential steps. Author Locations, NPC Cards, and Information Cards separately, then cross-link them.

=== 6a: Write Locations

For each location, fill in:

[horizontal]
Name:: Location name.
Description:: What the agents see when they arrive.
Availability:: Access condition — *Open*, *Clue-locked*, *Contact-locked*, *Time-locked*, or *Packet-locked*.
NPCs Present:: Which NPCs can be found here (may change at milestones).
Information Available:: Information IDs (I#) discoverable at this location.
Organizations Present:: Which organizations have interest or presence here (O# shorthand).
Positive Result:: What agents gain from a successful engagement — Information IDs, NPC access, new locations.
Negative Result:: What happens if the scene goes badly — organization advances, tails, closures.
Milestone Changes:: How this location changes at specific milestones (e.g., "When O4M2: Suárez pulled off case").

.Build 5–8 Locations for a standard case:

* 2–3 should be *Open* at case start
* The rest should unlock through investigation, NPC introductions, or packet returns
* The final confrontation / containment location should require at least 2 prior unlocks

=== 6b: Write NPC Cards

For each significant NPC, create a playing-card-sized reference card:

[horizontal]
Name:: NPC name.
Organization:: Affiliated organization (O#), faction, or independent.
Secret:: Something they are hiding from the agents.
Goal:: What they are actively trying to accomplish during this case.
Artifact Connection:: How they encountered the artifact and what effect proximity has had.
Starting Knowledge:: Information IDs (I#) they know at case start.
Gained Knowledge:: Information learned at milestones (e.g., "At O2M2: knows handler name — I7").
Locations:: Where they can be found.
Positive Result:: What they share if approached well — Information IDs, introductions, access.
Negative Result:: What happens if encounter goes badly — organization advance, tail, spooked witness.

=== 6c: Write Information Cards

For each discoverable fact, create a playing-card-sized reference card:

[horizontal]
ID:: Shorthand identifier (I1, I2, etc.).
Content:: What the agents learn.
Found At:: Location IDs where this can be discovered.
Known By:: NPC IDs who possess this knowledge.
HQ Fallback:: Available from HQ at Phase X (prevents dead ends — costs shifts).
Type:: *Containment Truth* (essential) or *supporting intel* (useful but not required).

=== 6d: Cross-Link and Verify

After authoring all three entity types, verify the cross-links:

* Every *Found At* reference points to a real Location. Every *Known By* reference points to a real NPC Card.
* Every Containment Truth has at least *two field sources* (Locations + NPCs) plus an *HQ Fallback*.
* Every NPC's *Locations* field matches a real Location's *NPCs Present* field.
* Every Location's *Information Available* matches Information Cards' *Found At* entries.

.THE DEAD END TEST
****
For every Containment Truth, ask: _If the agents never visit one source, can they still learn this fact?_ If no, add another source or an earlier HQ Fallback phase.
****

---

== Step 7: Create Key NPCs

Every significant NPC needs three things:

[horizontal]
Secret:: Something they are hiding from the agents (not necessarily sinister).
Goal:: What they are actively trying to accomplish *right now* during this case.
Artifact Connection:: How they encountered the artifact and what effect proximity has had on them.

.Build 4–8 NPCs for a standard case:

* At least 1 potential ally / quiet source
* At least 1 rival faction operative
* At least 1 civilian or institutional figure caught in the middle
* At least 1 viable bearer candidate if the artifact selects carriers

.TIP
****
Ask: _If the agents were not present, what would this NPC do?_ If the answer is nothing, the NPC is an information dispenser. Cut them or give them an active agenda.
****

---

== Step 8: Set Up the Shift Timeline

The shift timeline is the operational calendar. Prepare a tracker showing:

* *10 days* (default), *4 shifts per day*: Morning, Day, Evening, Night
* Mark any *known windows* — handoff deadlines, event nights, transport schedules
* Leave space for *packet send/return markers*
* Note any *time-locked scenes* or *organization triggers* tied to specific shifts

This handout is for coordination — share it with the table. Do *not* put your hidden organization values on it.

---

== Step 9: Prepare Packet Guidance

The agents can send one Covenant Request Packet per shift. Before play, prepare:

* 3–5 *likely packet requests* based on the case's available leads
* The *minimum response time* (default is 2 shifts; sensitive requests take 3+)
* What each response *contains* — a truth, a contact, a scene unlock, or a theory confirmation
* Whether any packet can be *intercepted, delayed, or partially wrong*

Packets should sharpen the next decision, not solve the case.

---

== Step 10: Assemble the Case File Package

Gather your work from the previous steps into the complete case file package (see <<case-file-package>> in Chapter 15):

. *Case File* (player brief) — Write the in-world mission brief containing only what the Covenant would share: relic surface description, region, timeline pressure, known organizations, starting NPC dossiers, and regional contacts. Include the Case ID (`VC-[Region Code]-[Year]-[Number]`), region, format (session count), shift cadence, and core problem statement.
. *Case Brief* (DA only) — Write your mystery statement and DA notes: what is actually going on, who is really involved, and how the case resolves.
. *Operations Board* — Completed in Steps 4–5.
. *Organization Reference* — List all organizations with their O#M# milestone descriptions and cross-link instructions.
. *Relic Sheet* — Full artifact profile from Step 1: tier, category, activation condition, mechanical effects, fracture table, containment profile, and containment truth checklist.
. *Location Pages* — One per Location from Step 6a.
. *NPC Cards* — One per NPC from Steps 6b and 7.
. *Information Cards* — One per information entry from Step 6c, double-sided (player-facing front, DA reference back).

---

== Quick Reference: The Solvability Checklist

Before you run the case, verify:

[%interactive]
* [ ] Every Containment Truth has at least two field sources (Locations + NPCs) plus an HQ fallback.
* [ ] No single Location is mandatory for forward motion.
* [ ] Every Location reveals information, unlocks an NPC or route, or worsens the situation informatively.
* [ ] At least one actionable option is visible at the start of each shift.
* [ ] A failed scene produces movement, not dead air.
* [ ] The Mystery Statement fits in one sentence.
* [ ] Each organization has player-facing signs before it becomes a crisis.
* [ ] Each NPC has a secret, a goal, and an artifact connection.

---

== Quick Reference: Session Flow

Each shift at the table follows this procedure:

. *Review visible pressure signs.* State what changed since the last shift.
. *Choose the team's primary undertaking.* Scene action, travel, surveillance, research, preparation, or recovery.
. *Optionally send one Covenant Request Packet.*
. *Resolve the undertaking.* Play the scene or handle the shift action.
. *Advance consequences.* On the Operations Board, advance any organization rows triggered by time, noise, or mishandling. Fill in the next shift quadrant on the phases row and check for relic milestones crossed this shift.
. *Mark delayed returns.* Note when packet responses or stakeout results will arrive.

When a shift becomes an active scene, resolve it as a bundle:

. State the objective.
. You state the Key Activity.
. Each player declares a unique contribution (Immediate, Shield, Position, or Delayed).
. Roll what matters.
. Disclose results in bands: Clean Progress, Progress With Cost, Partial Progress, Setback With Signal, or Breach.
