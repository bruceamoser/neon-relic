Case Name The Spear That Went Dark
Region Buenos Aires, Argentina

I — Situation Summary
What the Covenant knows. Brief the agents on the operational situation.
Three nights ago, a professional theft crew breached a private vault beneath the Ferreyra estate in Buenos Aires. The vault held an artifact of significant concern — a relic historically associated with sovereignty, divine mandate, and mass persuasion. The theft crew fractured before completing their planned handoff. The artifact is now loose in Buenos Aires. Local police are investigating the break-in as an art theft. At least one other organization is running a parallel recovery. The Covenant needs the artifact located and returned to containment before it reaches a buyer or activates in the field.
II — Agent Objectives
Primary Objective
Locate the artifact and establish Covenant custody. Learn whatever containment protocols are needed to transport it safely.
Secondary Objective
Prevent the artifact from leaving the country. Maintain operational secrecy. Identify any individuals who may have been psychologically affected by proximity to the relic.
III — Known Organizations
Organizations the Covenant has identified as active or potentially involved.
Buenos Aires Metropolitan Police — investigating the vault breach as a residential robbery. Likely to encounter agents at the estate or in the neighbourhood. The Church (local diocese) — the Ferreyra vault had historical Church connections. Expect clerical interest in the artifact’s recovery. Unknown Buyers — intelligence suggests the theft was commissioned, not opportunistic. Someone is expecting delivery. The Ferreyra Estate — the custodian family. Cooperative but frightened. May have partial knowledge of the artifact’s handling requirements.

V — Starting Leads
Available locations, lines of inquiry, or contacts the agents can pursue immediately.
The Ferreyra Estate — the crime scene. Police have processed it but may not have understood what they were looking at. Lucia Ferreyra is willing to meet. The local police station — Detective Suárez has physical evidence and witness statements. Access requires cover or leverage. Church archives — historical records of the artifact’s custody chain may exist in the diocesan archive.
VI — Operational Parameters
Timeline Pressure
HIGH. The artifact is active and escalating. Covenant analysts estimate the situation will become uncontainable within days, not weeks. Every shift matters.
Constraints
Maintain cover identities. Do not reveal Covenant existence to local authorities. Minimize civilian exposure to the artifact. Standard Rules of Engagement apply — lethal force only to protect life.
VII — Regional Contacts
Covenant assets, safe houses, or local contacts in the operational area.
HQ will provide standard Covenant Request Packet support. No permanent safe house in Buenos Aires — secure a hotel or short-let under cover names. Emergency extraction via the port is available as a last resort.
