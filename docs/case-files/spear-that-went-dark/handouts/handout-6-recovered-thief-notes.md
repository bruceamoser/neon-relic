# Image Prompt — Handout 6: Recovered Thief Notes

Copy the entire prompt below into an image generator. Output filename: `spear-handout-6-recovered-thief-notes.png`

---

Create a single full-page portrait image of recovered criminal field notes from 1987 Buenos Aires, written on a wrinkled notebook page or torn loose sheet of lined paper. The handwriting should be frantic ballpoint pen in blue or black ink, with uneven spacing, hurried letter formation, crossed-out phrases, pressure marks where the pen dug in, and occasional corrections squeezed between lines. The content should suggest a criminal participant whose plan is falling apart: references to a meeting location, a partner who did not show, strange physical symptoms near the object, a sense that something is wrong with the relic itself, and an abrupt trailing-off as though the writer stopped mid-thought. Include realistic physical damage: two or three fold creases, thumb grime along one edge, light paper yellowing, a small tear at one corner, and minimal restrained smudging where the writer's hand dragged across wet ink. The page should feel panicked, damaged, and human — a desperate person writing under stress — but still legible enough to function as a game handout. Use late-1980s found-document realism: cheap notebook paper, ordinary ballpoint pen, analog photography or flatbed-scan aesthetic. No modern notebook branding, no gel pens, no designer stationery, no blood-soaked shock effects, no fantasy parchment, no occult calligraphy, no supernatural burn marks, no horror-movie staging. The finished image should look like real evidence, not artwork about evidence.
