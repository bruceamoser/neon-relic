# Image Prompt — Relic: The Spear of Destiny

Copy the entire prompt below into an image generator. Output filename: `spear-relic-spear-of-destiny.png`

---

Create a single full-page portrait image of the Spear of Destiny as a recovered artifact photograph — a forensic or cataloging-style image of the relic laid on a dark velvet or lead-lined cloth inside an open containment case. The spear is an ancient iron spearhead mounted on a restored wooden shaft assembly, roughly full-arm length. The spearhead is dark corroded iron with visible hammer marks, a central binding of gold or silver wire wrapping the blade to the shaft, and a small inset pin or nail fragment secured beneath the binding — the traditional relic element. The shaft is dark aged wood, partially wrapped in stained leather cord, with a metal ferrule at the base. The object should look old, heavy, and real — not a fantasy weapon but an archaeological artifact that has been repaired, venerated, and hidden across centuries. The containment case around it is lined with lead foil and packed with ash-dusted cloth. The lighting is clinical — a single overhead lamp or flash, as in an evidence or museum-storage photograph, casting sharp shadows. A small paper tag or handwritten label is visible at one edge. The overall tone should be documentary and unsettling — this object is significant not because it looks magical but because it looks like something people have killed and died for. Use a late-1980s analog photography aesthetic: flash-on-dark-background, muted tones, visible grain, evidence-photo flatness. No fantasy glow, no magical aura, no ornate decoration, no clean museum display, no digital styling, no supernatural effects.
