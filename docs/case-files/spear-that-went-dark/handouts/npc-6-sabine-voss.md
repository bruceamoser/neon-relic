Create a single portrait-oriented character illustration of Sabine Voss, a Vantablack Compact regional handler in her mid-forties. She is controlled, elegant, transactional, and dangerous without ever appearing violent. Her face is angular and composed with cool light eyes, precise makeup, and an expression of measured patience — a woman accustomed to managing assets, not explaining herself. She wears a tailored dark blazer over a silk blouse, understated gold jewelry, and carries herself like someone who belongs in expensive hotel lobbies and consulate anterrooms. One hand rests on a leather attaché case or the back of a chair. The background should suggest an upscale Buenos Aires hotel suite or private club — polished dark wood, a heavy curtain, a crystal ashtray, muted ambient light from a shaded lamp. The overall tone should be realistic, cold, and subtly menacing — this is a woman who finances theft crews and calls it procurement. Use a late-1980s analog portrait aesthetic: cool tungsten tones, soft shadows, restrained color palette, slight grain. No fantasy elements, no glowing effects, no supernatural imagery, no action poses, no modern styling, no femme-fatale clichés.
