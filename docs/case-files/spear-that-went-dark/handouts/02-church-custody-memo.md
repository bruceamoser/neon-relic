# Church Custody Memorandum

## For Restricted Circulation
Custodial review regarding the lance relic currently associated with the Buenos Aires family vault.

The family in question should not be described as owners. They were temporary lay custodians under an arrangement established after wartime displacement and never publicly regularized. The arrangement was tolerated because open acknowledgment would have created political, doctrinal, and diplomatic consequences no authority wished to inherit.

The object has always presented three linked dangers:
- scandal of authenticity
- political misuse
- devotional catastrophe

Past custodians reported that direct handling without procedural silence produced immediate changes in rhetoric among nearby witnesses. Speech patterns shifted first. Men spoke as if command had returned to them. Existing grievances became providential.

The older black-chamber recommendation was clear:
- no crowds
- no acclamation
- no blood near the object
- no use of triumphal or imperial titles in the containment zone
- no transfer without liturgical sequence and material shielding

If the sequence has been broken, reclamation by force alone is contraindicated. A broken chain of custody may inflame the object rather than secure it.
