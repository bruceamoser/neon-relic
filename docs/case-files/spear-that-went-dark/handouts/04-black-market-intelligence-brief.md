# Black-Market Intelligence Brief

## Distribution
Counterintelligence / Recovery / Need-to-Know

## Subject
Buyer interest cluster surrounding lance-class Christian war relic in Southern Cone transit channels.

## Executive Summary
The object is attractive to at least three buyer types:
- prestige collectors seeking rarity and deniability
- militant networks seeking symbolic legitimacy and propaganda value
- intelligence intermediaries interested in coercive, ritual, or psychological applications

## Why the market cares
The relic is valuable even before authenticity is settled. Competing myths increase value. The most committed buyers do not require museum-grade proof. They require a plausible chain, symbolic usability, and enough fear around the object to make rivals hesitate.

## Current market behavior
- The Compact is spending aggressively to contain embarrassment and recover control of the sale narrative.
- Secondary brokers are asking for proof-of-custody, not scholarly verification.
- At least one foreign network is willing to accept the object if staged as a movement relic rather than a devotional artifact.

## Operational Warning
If photographs circulate showing the relic in a charged ritual, military, or nationalist context, the sale window will narrow and intensify. After that point, recovery becomes less about interception and more about interrupting a myth in motion.
