# Image Prompt — Handout 5: Tabloid Clipping

Copy the entire prompt below into an image generator. Output filename: `spear-handout-5-tabloid-clipping.png`

---

Create a single full-page portrait image of a sensational late-1980s tabloid newspaper clipping from Buenos Aires reporting on blackout trails, a robbery aftermath, bleeding witnesses, and public religious panic. The clipping should have a loud bold headline in large type, a subheadline or deck line adding detail, two or three dense text columns in small newsprint type, and a halftone photo box or low-quality inset image area showing an indistinct street scene or building exterior. The paper should be cheap newsprint with visible grain, slight yellowing, and ink absorption texture. The edges of the clipping should be slightly torn or scissor-cut, as though removed from a full broadsheet by hand. Include minor real-world artifacts: a faint crease where the clipping was folded for filing, a small smudge or fingerprint shadow, and barely visible text bleed-through from the reverse side. The tone should be urgent, commercially exploitative, and public-facing — a real paper trying to sell panic and coincidence — without tipping into parody, meme exaggeration, or horror-poster composition. Use authentic late-1980s tabloid print production: no desktop-publishing cleanliness, no full-color modern design, no contemporary photojournalism sharpness, no digital layout, no fantasy effects, no supernatural glow. The finished image should look like a disposable period clipping a game master could hand to a player as evidence.
