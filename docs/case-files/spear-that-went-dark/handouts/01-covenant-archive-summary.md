# Covenant Archive Summary

## Subject
Potential Longinus-class relic in black custody circulation, Buenos Aires channel, postwar transfer lineage compromised.

## Archive Classification
Keep / Catalogers / Restricted Custodial Sequence Material

## Working Assessment
The object locally described as `the Spear` should not be treated as a devotional relic alone. Prior Covenant notes classify this family of objects as mandate relics: items that bind wound trauma to political legitimacy. Their danger lies less in simple curse behavior and more in their ability to convert belief, blood, and command into one self-reinforcing loop.

## Known Historical Pattern
- Relic surfaces near periods of political fracture or succession anxiety.
- Carriers quickly develop conviction that history requires violent completion.
- Witnesses rationalize surrounding violence as justified, fated, or sacred.
- Custody breaks are more dangerous than long-term sealed storage.

## Handling Notes
- Do not permit public language of destiny, triumph, empire, holy rule, or chosen bloodline around the object.
- Isolate any current carrier from followers, cameras, and symbolic staging.
- Establish substitute custody protocol before transport.
- Expect ideological contagion before visible paranormal effects.

## Archive Warning
This item class does not merely corrupt. It authorizes. Field teams that approach it as contraband rather than claim-object have historically lost control of the human terrain first.
