= Sample Case File: The Spear That Went Dark

This chapter presents a complete sample case built for the revised Neon Relic case structure. Rather than reproducing every NPC card, Location form, and Information Card inline, the chapter focuses on *design commentary* — why the case is built the way it is, what makes it work, and what a DA should understand before running it.

The full case packet — filled-in forms for every component of the 8-piece document package — lives in `docs/case-files/spear-that-went-dark/`. Print or reference those files at the table. Read this chapter to understand the design logic behind them.

== Case Summary

[%header,cols="2,4"]
|===
| Case ID | VC-AR-87-041
| Region | Buenos Aires, Argentina
| Format | 1-3 sessions
| Primary Rival | The Vantablack Compact
| Secondary Pressure | Local law enforcement, Church custodians, foreign buyers
| Shift Cadence | 4 shifts per day
| Packet Response Floor | 2 shifts
| Core Problem | A stolen relic has destabilized a black-market handoff and is pulling multiple factions toward one contaminated chain-of-custody crisis.
|===

== Working Premise

The *Spear of Destiny* — or a relic credibly believed to be that spearhead and its restored shaft assembly — was held in a private vault beneath a fortified estate in Buenos Aires under discreet aristocratic custody. Three nights ago, the estate was hit by a professional theft crew funded through Vantablack Compact cutouts. The operation succeeded just long enough to become dangerous.

The theft team breached the estate, exposed the relic to a broken custody sequence, and failed to complete the scheduled handoff. Since then, the Spear has gone still somewhere in the city while hospitals, thieves, clergy, police, and black-market intermediaries all react to different pieces of the same event.

The agents arrive after the clean version of the crime is already over. What remains is a contaminated intelligence market circling an artifact that behaves like:

* a Christian wound relic
* an imperial mandate object
* a Cold War occult weapon

No faction understands all three at once.

== Agent Objectives

The team must:

. Locate the Spear before the Compact or foreign buyers recover its chain.
. Determine what interrupted the handoff.
. Learn the Spear's containment truths before direct recovery.
. Prevent export.
. Re-establish custody without triggering an urban threshold event.

== Design Commentary

This section explains the structural choices behind *The Spear That Went Dark*. Use it as a teaching guide for building your own cases.

=== Artifact as Tone Engine

The Spear does not merely threaten — it *authorizes*. Every design decision flows from this core behavior. The relic milestones escalate from subtle medical anomalies (Day 11: cruciform wounds) through environmental breakdown (Day 5: electronics fail) to a public sovereignty crisis (Day 1: mass claimant event). Each milestone is specific to the artifact's logic, not generic horror.

This specificity shapes the entire case. NPCs do not simply "act suspicious" — they begin speaking in command-language. Locations do not just "feel ominous" — radios fail and witnesses describe conviction rather than fear. When you design your own case, let the artifact's nature flavor every entity it touches.

=== Organization Architecture

The case uses six organizations — four Active, two Dormant. This is near the recommended maximum of eight rows and produces enough cross-pressure to fill a 1-3 session case without overwhelming the DA.

The four Active organizations represent distinct institutional responses to the same event:

* *O1 (The Vantablack Compact, start 9)* — the criminal apparatus that caused the problem
* *O2 (The Theft Crew, start 6)* — the unstable human debris from the heist, close to crisis
* *O3 (Buenos Aires Police, start 10)* — an institutional response the agents cannot control
* *O4 (The Catholic Church, start 12)* — a slow-moving rival with legitimate historical claims

Starting values are deliberately staggered. O2 starts at 6 — only six squares from catastrophe — making the surviving thief an urgent pressure source from the first day. O4 starts at 12, giving the Church time to observe before acting. This staggering ensures that organizations do not all hit milestones at the same time and that the DA always has at least one row that is about to escalate.

The two Dormant organizations — O5 (The Foreign Buyer) and O6 (Buenos Aires Press) — activate through specific triggers tied to other milestone consequences. This means the board grows more complex as the case progresses, which matches the feeling of a deteriorating situation.

=== Cross-Link Philosophy

Milestone consequences are the case's real pacing engine. When O2M2 fires (a wounded thief surfaces), it advances *both* O3 (police) and O1 (Compact) — because a visible thief attracts institutional and criminal attention simultaneously. This single milestone event produces two downstream pressure changes and opens a new Location (L5: Illicit Clinic).

Cross-links like this create the illusion of an intelligent, reactive world without requiring the DA to improvise connections in real time. They also prevent the case from stalling: even if agents ignore an organization, its milestones will eventually advance other rows, keeping the board in motion.

When designing your own cross-links, write them directly into milestone text: _"O2M2: A wounded thief surfaces. Advance O3 by 2 squares and O1 by 1 square."_ The DA reads the milestone description and executes — no arrows, no flowcharts, no separate cross-reference table.

=== Information Design

The case uses 22 Information Cards. Of those, 7 are Containment Truths — the facts agents _must_ discover for safe resolution. The remaining 15 are supporting intel that provides context, unlocks Locations, or identifies key NPCs.

The critical design constraint: every Containment Truth must be reachable from at least *two field sources* (any combination of Locations and NPCs) plus an *HQ Fallback* (a packet return on a specified day). This triple redundancy means no single failed scene, missed NPC, or bad roll can lock the agents out of victory.

For example, I9 (Custodians Not Owners) is available at L1 (Estate), L3 (Church Archive), and from three different NPCs (Lucia, Father Vale, Sister Inés). If the agents alienate every source, HQ can deliver it on Day 2. The truth is reachable from at least five different angles.

This redundancy does not make the case easy — it makes it *fair*. The difficulty comes from time pressure and organization escalation, not from information scarcity.

=== Location Gating

The seven Locations use a structured unlock pattern:

* *L1 (Estate)* — Open at case start. The guaranteed first scene.
* *L2–L6* — Clue-locked. Each unlocks through discoveries at prior Locations or from NPC interactions.
* *L7 (Hidden Shrine)* — Requires at least two prior clues. This is the endgame site.

This funnel shape ensures that agents always have something to investigate (L1 is open, and L2 and L3 unlock easily) while requiring genuine progress before reaching the final confrontation. The DA should never have a shift where the agents have zero available Locations.

=== Bearer as Tragic Figure

The case's strongest NPC — the bearer candidate (Iván Luján by default) — is designed as a tragedy, not a villain. He began as a Compact fixer and is becoming a prophet who believes his own myth. The Spear does not make soldiers; it makes claimants who believe history has chosen them.

This matters because it changes the endgame tone. If the bearer were simply an antagonist, the resolution would be a combat scene. Because the bearer is a person being consumed by a mandate relic, the resolution should feel like an exorcism, an intervention, or a containment crisis — not a gunfight.

=== Pacing Guidance

For a single-session case, advance organizations aggressively: 2-3 squares per shift on the most active row. The agents should feel the board tightening around them.

For a multi-session case, let the first session establish the situation (the estate, the police, the Church trail) and the second session accelerate the crisis (the thief surfaces, the Compact deploys professionals, the bearer emerges).

The day countdown exists to create windows of opportunity, not to punish slow players. If the agents are paralyzed, give them a sign from the next organization milestone. If they are moving too fast, let a packet return land with partial information that redirects rather than confirms.

== DA Quick Summary

*What happened?* The Compact funded the theft. The crew breached the estate. The Spear destabilized the transfer. The handoff failed. The relic is now hidden, active, and pulling multiple factions toward one contaminated custody crisis.

*What do the agents need?* Where the crew broke apart, who now has proximity control, what the old containment procedure was, and how to move the relic without completing its escalation.

*What makes the case work?* The object is simultaneously sacred relic, imperial mandate, and weaponizable asset. Each faction understands only one slice of that truth, and the agents must assemble all three before the city becomes the ritual site.
