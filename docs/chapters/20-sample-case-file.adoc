= Sample Case File: The Spear That Went Dark

Three nights ago, someone broke into the fortified Ferreyra estate in Buenos Aires and stole a relic that had been sitting in a ritual vault since the end of the Second World War. The containment sequence — a liturgical suppression rite maintained for four decades by a succession of aristocratic custodians and discreet Church intermediaries — was shattered the moment the vault door came open. The theft crew expected a black-market handoff. What they got was a fractured chain of custody, a wounded colleague, a Compact fixer who is beginning to hear voices of divine mandate, and a church hierarchy that is quietly running its own parallel recovery.

The agents arrive after the clean version of the crime is already over. What remains is a contaminated intelligence market circling an artifact that is simultaneously sacred relic, imperial mandate, and weaponizable asset. No faction understands all three at once. The agents have to.

This chapter introduces the sample case that ships with Neon Relic. The full case file package — every filled-in form, every NPC Card, every Location page, every Information Card — lives in the starter kit under `sample-case-file/`. Print those documents. Spread them on your table. Use them exactly as written for your first session, or read through them to see how the system works before building your own.

What follows here is not a second copy of those documents. It is the story of the case — the narrative a DA needs to internalize before sitting down to run it — followed by the visual handouts and portraits you will use at the table.

---

== The Situation

The *Spear of Destiny* — or a relic credibly identified as that spearhead and its restored shaft assembly — was held in a private vault beneath the Ferreyra estate under a postwar custody arrangement with the Catholic Church. The vault was not merely a safe. It was a ritual containment site, maintained through a liturgical suppression sequence that had to be performed on a precise schedule by authorized custodians. Three nights ago, a professional theft crew breached the vault, broke the containment lineage, and failed to complete the buyer handoff that was supposed to follow.

Since then, the Spear has gone still somewhere in the city. The heist crew has fractured — one member is suffering from wound bloom at an off-grid clinic, another has vanished, and the surviving leader is trying to fence the object through a currency exchange with connections to foreign buyers. The Vantablack Compact, which funded the theft through cutouts, has deployed a regional fixer named Iván Luján to recover the asset. Luján is becoming the strongest bearer candidate — the Spear does not make soldiers, it makes prophets who believe their own myth. Meanwhile, the Buenos Aires police are building a robbery case they do not fully understand, and the Church is quietly sending its own people to get the relic back.

The agents enter this mess somewhere around Day 14 on the Operations Board. They have roughly two weeks before the Spear finishes selecting a claimant and the city becomes the ritual site.

=== What the Agents Need

Four things, in rough order of discovery:

. *Where the crew broke apart* — what happened after the theft, where the pieces landed.
. *Who has proximity control* — who is physically closest to the Spear right now and how close they are to bearer selection.
. *The old containment protocol* — what the liturgical suppression sequence was, what materials are needed to replicate it, and who still knows the procedure.
. *A recovery plan* — how to move the Spear from its current location to Covenant custody without completing its escalation.

=== What Makes This Case Work

The Spear is the center of every decision in the case, but it is not a simple MacGuffin. It has its own behavior — it authorizes. Each relic milestone on the Operations Board represents a specific escalation tied to the Spear's nature: cruciform wounds appearing on handlers, electronics failing in proximity, witnesses speaking in command-language, and ultimately, a public sovereignty event. Every NPC, every Location, and every organization row is flavored by what the Spear does to the people near it.

The six organizations create layered cross-pressure. The theft crew is close to collapse (starting value 6 — only six squares from catastrophe). The police are building slowly (starting value 10). The Church moves deliberately (starting value 12). The Compact is aggressive (starting value 9). Two dormant organizations — the foreign buyer and the Buenos Aires press — activate through milestone triggers, making the board more complex as the situation deteriorates.

The cross-links are the real pacing engine. When the wounded thief surfaces (O2M2), it advances both the police and the Compact — because a visible thief attracts institutional and criminal attention simultaneously. These ripple effects create the feeling of an intelligent, reactive world without requiring the DA to improvise connections at the table.

== Running the Case

If this is your first Neon Relic case, run it exactly as written. Print the case file package from the starter kit, read the Case Brief, study the Operations Board, and familiarize yourself with the NPC Cards and Location pages. You do not need to memorize everything — the forms are designed to be consulted during play, not recited from memory.

For pacing guidance:

* *Single session:* Advance organizations aggressively — two to three squares per shift on the most active row. The agents should feel the board tightening around them from the start.
* *Multi-session:* Let the first session establish the situation — the estate, the police, the Church trail. Let the second session accelerate the crisis — the thief surfaces, the Compact deploys, the bearer emerges.

The day countdown creates windows of opportunity, not a punishment for slow play. If the agents are paralyzed, give them a sign from the next organization milestone. If they are moving too quickly, let a packet return with partial information that redirects rather than confirms.

== DA Quick Summary

*What happened?* The Compact funded the theft. The crew breached the estate. The Spear destabilized the transfer. The handoff failed. The relic is now hidden, active, and pulling multiple factions toward one contaminated custody crisis.

*What do the agents need?* Where the crew broke apart, who now has proximity control, what the old containment procedure was, and how to move the relic without completing its escalation.

*What makes the case work?* The object is simultaneously sacred relic, imperial mandate, and weaponizable asset. Each faction understands only one slice of that truth, and the agents must assemble all three before the city becomes the ritual site.
