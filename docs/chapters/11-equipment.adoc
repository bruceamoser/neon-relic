= 1980s Equipment and Analog Gear

Navigating the neon-lit streets and abandoned industrial sectors of the 1980s requires specialized equipment. Items provide a *Gear Bonus* (extra black d6 dice) to specific skill rolls. Pushing a roll can cause the item to break, temporarily reducing its bonus.

To reflect the covert nature of the Covenant, characters do not use personal cash. Instead, they utilize their *Clearance Level (CL)* during the Equipping Phase to requisition supplies from Stack. Items are categorized by their required CL.

== Encumbrance

Every agent can carry a limited amount of gear before it slows them down. Encumbrance (*Enc.*) is listed for each item in the equipment tables above.

=== Encumbrance Scale

[%header,cols="^1,3"]
|===
| Enc. | Examples

| ½ | Small concealable items: a pocket recorder, lockpick kit, walkie-talkie.
| 1 | Standard field gear: first aid kit, camera, crowbar, pistol.
| 2 | Heavy or bulky gear: trauma surgical kit, thermal camera, shotgun.
| 3+ | Oversized or special items: heavy weapons, large artifact containment cases.
|===

=== Carry Capacity

An agent's carry capacity is *Strength × 2* Enc. points.

Wearing armor adds its CL to your total Enc. (Concealed Kevlar = +1; Tactical Riot Armor = +2).

=== Encumbered

If your total Enc. exceeds your carry capacity, you gain the *Encumbered* Condition (see `docs/chapters/06-health-damage-armor.adoc`):

* −1 die on all Strength and Agility rolls.
* Moving to a new zone costs a *Slow Action* instead of a Fast Action.

You remain Encumbered until your load drops back within capacity — drop gear, stash it in the car, or pass it to a teammate.

=== Overloaded

If your Enc. exceeds *Strength × 3*, you are *Overloaded*:

* You *cannot move to a different zone* at all.
* At the GM's discretion, you cannot take Fast Actions for movement.
* You must drop excess gear before you can move.

> *Overloaded is not a formal Condition* — it is a hard stop. You simply cannot move until weight is reduced.

=== Clearance Levels (CL) and Requisition

Every item lists a *Clearance Level (CL)* requirement. Agents requisition gear during the *Equipping Phase* between Case Files through their Division's Stack contact. Your character's current CL determines which items you can request:

[%header,cols="^1,3"]
|===
| CL | Who Can Access

| 1 | All agents — basic field supplies.
| 2 | Agents with at least one completed Case File, or Division default.
| 3 | Agents with field commendations or specific Division clearance.
| 4 | Senior agents and Warden rank — restricted hardware and experimental tech.
|===

Clearance is a *narrative resource* — the GM may escalate or revoke access based on the agent's standing with the Covenant (see Division Standing rules).
