= Notable Historical Covenant Members

Because of the rigid structure of 441 members, players are part of an unbroken lineage of historical stewards. The following represent legendary past members from the Covenant archives.

== The Medieval & Renaissance Eras

[%header,cols="3,1,1,5"]
|===
| Member | Dates | Division | Legacy

| *Godfrey of Bouillon* | c. 1060–1100 | Recovery | Used the chaos of the First Crusade to seize and sequester dangerous Levantine relics.
| *Hildegard of Bingen* | 1098–1179 | Keep | A visionary nun who created early containment wards disguised as complex musical compositions.
| *Albertus Magnus* | c. 1200–1280 | Wayfinder | Dominican friar who mapped the alchemical resonance of European artifacts.
| *Roger Bacon* | c. 1219–1292 | Keep | Designed the first lead-lined alchemical containment chambers, hidden within academic manuscripts.
| *Marco Polo* | 1254–1324 | Recovery | Used his famous travels as a smokescreen to extract dangerous Silk Road artifacts.
| *Nicolas Flamel* | 1330–1418 | Keep | Guarded a highly unstable, reality-warping artifact masked as the "philosopher's stone."
| *Marsilio Ficino* | 1433–1499 | Wayfinder | Translated the _Corpus Hermeticum_ to decipher ancient artifact triggers for field agents.
| *Heinrich Cornelius Agrippa* | 1486–1535 | Keep | Authored the _Three Books of Occult Philosophy_ as a coded manual for Covenant catalogers.
| *Paracelsus* | 1493–1541 | Wayfinder | Pioneered treatments for the physical symptoms of early-stage Corruption Cascades.
| *John Dee* | 1527–1608 | Wayfinder | Used Enochian magic and Elizabeth I's court to intercept Spanish occult weaponry.
|===

== The Enlightenment & Victorian Eras

[%header,cols="3,1,1,5"]
|===
| Member | Dates | Division | Legacy

| *Athanasius Kircher* | 1602–1680 | Keep | Categorized cursed Egyptian artifacts and built the first deep-subterranean vaults beneath Rome.
| *Emanuel Swedenborg* | 1688–1772 | Wayfinder | Mapped the ethereal plane, developing techniques to bypass supernatural traps.
| *Count of St. Germain* | 1712–1784 | Recovery | Used his alleged "immortality" as cover for a generational lineage of Recovery agents.
| *Giovanni Battista Belzoni* | 1778–1823 | Recovery | Posed as an archaeologist to extract cursed Egyptian antiquities from collapsing tombs.
| *Sir Richard Francis Burton* | 1821–1890 | Recovery | Master of disguise and linguistics who infiltrated forbidden cities to retrieve stolen relics.
| *Heinrich Schliemann* | 1822–1890 | Recovery | Used the excavation of Troy as a front to extract weaponized mythological artifacts.
| *Helena Petrovna Blavatsky* | 1831–1891 | Wayfinder | Normalized the esoteric through the Theosophical Society to enable covert investigations.
| *William Wynn Westcott* | 1848–1925 | Keep | Co-founded the Golden Dawn, using lodges as safehouses for psychological ward development.
| *Arthur Edward Waite* | 1857–1942 | Wayfinder | Encoded artifact locations and entity weaknesses into the Rider-Waite Tarot deck.
| *William Butler Yeats* | 1865–1939 | Wayfinder | Used poetic resonance and automatic writing to locate dormant Celtic artifacts.
|===
