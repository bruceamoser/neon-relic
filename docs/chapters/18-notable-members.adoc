= Notable Historical Covenant Members

The Covenant's archives contain centuries of operational records, personnel files, and after-action reports. These records are not public — they are locked in the Keep's Restricted Stack and accessed only by agents with appropriate standing or on specific Wayfinder authorization.

What follows is a curated excerpt from those archives: notable members from across the Covenant's history, organized by era. These are not myths. Each entry represents a documented agent who operated under the same mandate agents hold today — discover, recover, and contain. The artifacts they handled were real. So were the costs.

*Historical records as gameplay:* The DA may surface these entries during play when agents visit the Keep's archive, consult a Wayfinder analyst, or request historical precedent on a specific artifact type. Each entry can serve as a seed for a mission briefing, a clue about a current artifact's lineage, or an NPC hook (a descendant, a rival organization's historical target, a still-active custodian network).

*On terminology:* The Covenant's internal documentation predates current field terminology. Entries may reference "cascades" (now called Corruption), "ethereal mapping" (Lore-based investigations), "warding compositions" (early Containment Rituals), and "resonance fields" (artifact emission signatures). These terms are historical artifacts of the archive's era, not current mechanical terms. See xref:chapters/08-corruption-fear-healing.adoc#chapter-corruption[Ch 8 — Corruption] and xref:chapters/11-artifacts.adoc#chapter-artifacts[Ch 11 — Artifacts] for current terminology.

== Using Historical Records in Play

The DA may introduce archive entries as in-play resources. Some uses:

* *Containment precedent:* If agents encounter an artifact with no known Containment Profile, a Keep archivist may surface a historical case matching the artifact's tier or behavioral signature. This does not provide the full Containment Truth but reduces the Difficulty of Lore (WIT) rolls by 1.

* *Historical contact networks:* Several entries reference institutional cover operations (universities, lodges, churches). Their successor organizations may still maintain partial awareness of Covenant activity — useful as civilian Contacts with historical knowledge.

* *Rival faction provenance:* Historical operations often left artifacts in circulation. If an artifact in a current case has a recorded lineage, the Wayfinder archive entry may reveal that a rival faction (Compact, MJ-12, Consortium) was tracking it decades earlier.

* *Arc seeds:* A specific historical agent’s unresolved case may form the backbone of a campaign arc. "What Parsons didn’t destroy in 1952 resurfaced in Buenos Aires last week" is a complete briefing opening.
